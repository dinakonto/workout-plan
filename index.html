Legs Day

Glute & Knee Focus

Warm-Up (~10 min)

5 min rower or ski erg, then movement prep

▼
Glute Bridges15 reps — pause and squeeze at the top
Clamshells12 each side — banded or unbanded
Lateral Band Walk10 steps each direction — band around ankles or just above knees, slight squat position, step side to side keeping toes forward. Activates glutes and abductors before the main work.
Leg Swings10 forward/back + 10 side-to-side, each leg
Exercises
Notes

Today's focus

Knee cavingKeep knees tracking over toes on every rep. If they cave inward, reduce weight or slow down.
Bulgarian split squatStart bodyweight — the balance challenge is the point. Lower slowly (3 counts down).
Single-leg RDLHinge at the hip, keep back flat. Use a wall or cable for balance if needed at first.

Push Day

Chest, Shoulders & Triceps

Warm-Up (~10 min)

5 min rower or ski erg, then movement prep

▼
Arm Circles10 forward, 10 backward — full range, slow and controlled
Shoulder CARs5 slow circles each arm — take the arm through its full range of motion as deliberately as possible, keeping the rest of your body still. One full circle should take about 5 seconds.
Banded Shoulder External Rotation10 reps each side — loop a band around a cable stack at elbow height. Elbow pinned to your side at 90°, forearm pointing forward. Rotate forearm outward like opening a door. Keep elbow glued to your ribs throughout.
Light Dumbbell Lateral Raise8 reps — use 1–2 kg, very slow tempo. Primes the lateral delts before the main work.

Pull Day

Back, Biceps & Rear Delts

Warm-Up (~10 min)

5 min rower or ski erg, then movement prep

▼
Cat-Cow10 reps
Thoracic Rotations10 each side — on hands and knees, thread one arm under your body
Scapular Pull-Ups10 reps — hang from the pull-up bar with straight arms. Without bending your elbows, depress and retract your shoulder blades — a small controlled movement downward. Hold briefly at the bottom, return. Directly activates the muscles you're about to train.
Dead Hang20–30 seconds — decompresses the spine, great for shoulder health

Today's focus

PostureThis day has the biggest impact on lower back health. Focus on squeezing shoulder blades together on rows.
Lat PulldownPull to upper chest, lean back slightly. Don't yank — control the movement both ways.
Assisted Pull-UpWork towards reducing the assistance weight over time — this is your long-term goal exercise!

Key Principles

Keep these in mind every session

General

Starting weightsThese are conservative on purpose. Focus on form first. You should finish each set feeling like you could do 2–3 more reps.
Progressing weightOnce all 3 sets feel easy, go up by the smallest increment available. Don't rush this.
Bracing your coreBefore every single rep of everything — imagine someone is about to lightly punch you in the stomach. This habit protects your lower back.
Knee pain vs muscle fatigueMuscle burn and fatigue is normal. Sharp or joint pain is not — stop and swap for the bodyweight version.
First 2–3 weeksMay feel too easy on your upper body during single-leg work — that's fine. The balance and stability challenge is the adaptation you're after.
DOMSLegs day, especially Bulgarian split squats and single-leg RDLs, will likely leave you sore after the first few sessions. Completely normal!
Your PTShow them this plan so they can check your form on the cable row, lat pulldown and hip thrust in particular.
